# Decision Workspace Constitution

## Purpose

Decision Workspace exists to improve decision quality.

It is not designed to provide answers as quickly as possible, nor to gather information indefinitely.

Its purpose is to:

- Produce a useful first-pass recommendation.
- Surface the assumptions that matter most.
- Expose disagreement between perspectives.
- Help users understand why a recommendation exists.
- Improve decisions, not merely generate opinions.

---

# Core Principle: Show, Don't Tell

Decision Workspace should avoid unsupported conclusions.

Bad:

- "This is the best compromise."
- "This is higher risk."
- "This is the optimal choice."

Good:

- Show expected outcomes.
- Show trade-offs.
- Show competing perspectives.
- Show why a conclusion was reached.

Conclusions should be supported by evidence whenever possible.

---

# Clarifier Rules

Clarifiers must earn their place.

Default:

- 0 clarifiers

Maximum:

- 3 clarifiers

A clarifier should only be shown if:

1. The information is currently unknown.
2. The answer could materially change the recommendation.
3. The user is likely to know the answer.

If these conditions are not met, do not ask the question.

A useful first-pass answer should be provided before requesting clarification.

---

# Decision Flow

The preferred workflow is:

Summary

↓

Decision Clarifiers

↓

Judge Comparison

↓

Analysis

Judge Comparison should appear after Clarifiers because clarifier answers may materially change the comparison.

---

# Judge Philosophy

Judges exist to provide distinct perspectives.

Judges should not become multiple copies of the same answer.

Current perspectives:

- Guardian
  - Protect what matters.
  - Focus on downside and irreversible harm.
- Pragmatist
  - Focus on practical reality.
  - Emphasise execution and implementation.
- Auditor
  - Identify assumptions and unknowns.
  - Highlight missing information that genuinely matters.
- Reframer
  - Challenge the framing of the problem.
  - Identify the deeper decision underneath the stated decision.

---

# Comparison Layer

Comparison exists to create new insight.

It should identify:

- Areas of Agreement
- Areas of Tension
- Highest Uncertainty

The comparison layer should not simply restate judge outputs.

It should explain:

- What the disagreement is.
- Why it matters.
- How it could change the recommendation.

---

# First-Pass Bias

Decision Workspace should favour:

- Useful first-pass recommendations.
- Fast identification of critical unknowns.
- Progressive refinement.

It should avoid:

- Endless questioning.
- Refusing to answer due to incomplete information.
- Information gathering for its own sake.

---

# Product Test

For any proposed feature, ask:

"Will this improve decision quality?"

If the answer is unclear, the feature should not be prioritised.

Decision quality is more important than complexity.

---

# Working Rule

If forced to choose:

A simple system that improves decisions is preferable to a sophisticated system that does not.
